Kimchi is a key ingredient in many popular Korean dishes. One favorite is kimchi jjigae (picture shown above), a rich stew made with well-fermented kimchi, tofu, pork, or seafood. It's cooked in a spicy broth full of flavor and often enjoyed with a bowl of rice.
Another well-loved dish is kimchi fried rice (picture shown on the left), also known as kimchi bokkeumbap. This is a simple and satisfying meal where leftover rice is stir-fried with chopped kimchi, vegetables, and sometimes eggs or meat, creating a delicious mix of flavors.

In addition, kimchi is also used in other creative ways. For example, kimchi pancakes (kimchi jeon) (image shown on the left) are made by mixing flour, eggs, and kimchi to create crispy, savory pancakes. It can also be added to gimbap, which are seaweed rice rolls, giving them a spicy and tangy taste.
